Artificial Intelligence and Autonomous Machines


An autonomous machine is a robot that performs behaviors or tasks with a high degree of autonomy.

Autonomous Machines that apply AI:

  • Driverless vehicles
  • Autonomous Weapon Systems
  • Autonomous Domestic Robots

Driverless cars

Driverless car uses Artificial Intelligence to interact with the environment, including other cars and predict the situation on the road in order to help a driver have a safe, fast and comfortable journey (Ford, 2009).

Fun fact: Since the start of testing self-driving cars in 2009 by companies, such as Uber and Google, there was only one lethal accident in March 2018. Hover to find out why did that happen! Pedestrian crossed the road illegally!

What are the benefits of driverless cars?
  • Safer (Waldrop, 2015)
  • More fuel-effective (Gonder et al, 2011)
  • Generally more effective than humans
  • Creating more workplaces (Peters, 2017)

Autonomous Weapon Systems

Autonomous Weapon Systems is a weapon system that, based on conclusions derived from gathered information and preprogrammed constraints, is capable of independently selecting and engaging targets (Crootof, 2015). Those are already used nowadays, for example, combat drones or automatic defensive systems.

Fun fact: The oldest automatically-triggered lethal weapon was invented in 1600. Hover to find out what is that! A land mine!


Autonomous Domestic Robots

Autonomous Domestic Robot is a machine used for household chores. There are many types of those: robotic vacuum cleaners, ironing robots, robotic kitchens, security robots, robotic lawn mowers, Mr. Handy, C3PO, Wall-E, etc., and all of them are using principles of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning. Let's just hope they don't take over the world.
